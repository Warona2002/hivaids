Symptoms of HIV

HIV symptoms can vary depending on the stage of the infection.
In the early stages, symptoms may be mild or absent, but as the disease progresses, more noticeable symptoms can occur.
Early detection is important for proper treatment.

Common Symptoms:

Fever

Fever is one of the most common early symptoms of HIV infection, especially in the first few weeks.

Fatigue

Chronic fatigue and feeling tired, even after rest, is common in people with HIV.

Unexplained Weight Loss

Significant, unexplained weight loss can be a symptom of HIV progressing into AIDS.

Skin Rashes

Rashes or skin conditions can be an early sign of HIV, especially in the acute phase of the infection.
